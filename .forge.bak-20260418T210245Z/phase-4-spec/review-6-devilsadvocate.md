## Summary

**VERDICT: PASS** (with 1 MAJOR concern tagged for Gate-2 awareness, 5 MINORs).

**Round-1 MAJOR resolution**:
- **f-devilsadvocate-1 (run_id)**: RESOLVED. `{issue_id}_{ISO8601}` is substantive, not cosmetic.
- **f-devilsadvocate-2 (tracker race)**: ACCEPTED AS DOCUMENTED. NOT_IN_SCOPE §6.19 + informational WARN — no algorithmic mitigation, by design.
- **f-devilsadvocate-3 (summary_table)**: RESOLVED. COST-R10 bounds ≤20 rows / ≤4 KB.
- **f-devilsadvocate-5 (/metrics dual-mode)**: RESOLVED. COST-R8/R11 force separate line items, ban cross-boundary grand total.
- **f-devilsadvocate-11 (trap ordering)**: RESOLVED. Trap is conditional on successful mkdir AND body checks ownership.

**Top 2 new concerns**:
1. **Cross-host-hint sidecar (round2-1, MAJOR)**: New file `$LOCK_DIR.cross-host-hint` introduced by revision is never GC'd, has its own read/write race, and the WARN fires only AFTER a cross-host race has happened. Recommend dropping it OR moving the detection into the mkdir-fail branch.
2. **run_id colons (round2-3, MINOR)**: ISO8601 with `:` is not filesystem-safe on NTFS and not URL-safe without percent-encoding. Suggest compact form `YYYYMMDDTHHMMSSZ`.

Other findings: awk `mktime` not universal on old BusyBox (round2-2), COST-R12 discovery test exits 0 on real failures (round2-4), single-host mitigation is post-hoc (round2-5), trap-body quoting is fragile for odd paths (round2-6).
